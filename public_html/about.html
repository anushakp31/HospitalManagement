The doctor serves a variety of functions in our society, including healer, civic leader, scientist, patient advocate, confidante, and role model. The modern doctor also serves as a steward of the environment, which is crucial. Doctors can do more to safeguard both human health and the planet's health, which are interdependent. Doctors must start combating  for concerns about the environment on a global scale. Doctors and surgeons deal with maintaining health as well as diagnosing and treating illnesses and injuries. Both clinical and nonclinical settings are used by doctors and surgeons in their work. Hospitals and doctor's offices are examples of clinical environments, while insurance firms, nonprofits, and governmental bodies are examples of nonclinical ones.

Outpatient surgery, also known as ambulatory surgery, day surgery day case surgery, or same-day surgery, is surgery that does not require an overnight hospital stay. The term “outpatient" arises from the fact that surgery patients may enter and leave the facility on the same day. The advantages of outpatient surgery over inpatient surgery include greater convenience and reduced costs.
